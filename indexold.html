About Me

I am a fourth-year combined masters and bachelor’s student (BSMS) in Computer Science at the Georgia Institute of Technology. I am broadly interested developing accessible, computationally efficient, and statistically principled approaches to acquiring actionable knowledge from data. Most problems I’ve worked on have had spatial-temporal flavor and address pertinent real-world societal and engineering challenges. Throughout my research work, I have developed and applied tools from kernel learning, information theory, representation learning, causal inference, econometrics, and mathematical programming (convex and integer).

Interests

Technical: Machine learning, causal inference, learning in spatial-temporal and sequential settings, statistics, optimization, scientific and technical computing

Applications: Mangagment Science and Service Operations, Business Analytics, Data Science for Policy, Biostatistics and Bioinformatics, Computational Chemistry
